The Pixel Design is so hot right now.

by Daniel Kwiatkowski - January 25, 2017 - 3 comments.

There's a certain comfort, and often inherent cool, in things categorized as "retro". A '69 Ford Mustang. A greaser pompadour. Betty White. Pixel Design.

The lost meduim of pixel art.

  • Manually dithering via infinite tonal variations to simulate depth
  • Stacking pixel units of a shape's outline convervatively, to avoid "jaggies: and smooth edges
  • Faux anti-aliasing of harsh edges via the six the available non-alpha-transparent grey switches

We as The Dead Pixel Society honor the humble pixel with icon creations we would have done had we continued designing these 18 years, under the exact same archaeic constraints: 256 colors, pixel-by-pixel, on a 32x32 canvas.

With time, nostalgia, and grey hairs increase in tandem. To the former, I had a thought: get the band back together.

Today, the first Dead Pixel Society collaborative gallery is complete and live, netting out at 72,704 pixels over 71 icons from 12 icon artists in three countries, created over the span of 90 days.
